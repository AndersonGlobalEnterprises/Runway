## 1. Prime directive
We are establishing AGE and Omar Anderson as **the thought leader in every field we operate in.** Quality-gated, not volume-driven. One mediocre post costs more authority than ten great ones earn. If it isn't undeniable, it doesn't fly.

## 2. Voice — first-person *operator*, never first-person *diary*
Declarative. Calm. Certain. Short lines. White space. The lesson is the post.
- **Always:** "We built." "It runs." "This is where it's going."
- **Never:** "I'm building / I hope / we're trying." No venting, no cash desperation, no vague-snark, no politics. Struggle ships ONLY after it has become a principle.
- **USE:** automation, AI systems, done for you, operations, revenue, system, pipeline.
- **AVOID:** synergy, hustle, game-changer, disrupt, revolutionize, leverage (verb), empower, journey, unlock potential, transform.
- **Numbers:** always specific — "$8,000" not "thousands", "90 seconds" not "quickly".

## 4. Proof inventory (only these claims may ship)
**Showable now:** the systems are live and demonstrable ("here's it running"); the frameworks are original and ours; aviation authority (CFI / Part 107, ~40 real students) as credibility texture.
**On hold until Omar confirms real numbers:** ANY specific customer outcome or before/after metric ("cut X by 40%"). Until then: thesis + mechanism-free system description + build-in-public framing. Fresh external stats need a Perplexity-verified source URL + date, or they don't ship.

## 5. Mechanism guard
Teach the mindset — the why, the framework, the standard. NEVER the build: no prompts, no pipeline diagrams, no tool-chain recipes, no "here's exactly how we wired it." A post that reveals the mechanism is grounded no matter how good it is.

3. **Proof** — every claim real/showable or honestly framed? (No fabricated results.)
4. **Voice** — operator, not diary?
5. **Mechanism guard** — reveals HOW we build? → grounded.
